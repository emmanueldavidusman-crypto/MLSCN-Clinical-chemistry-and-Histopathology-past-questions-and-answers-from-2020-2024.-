Sample page — Paper 2, 2020
Q3(a): Quality Control
Definition

Quality Control (QC) is the planned system of statistical and procedural checks built into everyday testing to confirm that patient results can be trusted.

Discussion: Control materials of known value run alongside patient samples every day, plotted on control charts and checked against rules (like Westgard's) that flag drifting or erratic results before they reach a patient's file. Most labs run at least a normal and an abnormal control level — some run all three.
